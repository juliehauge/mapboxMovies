Movies filmed in Oxford

102 Dalmatians

Oxford Prison was used for the scene in the film where Cruella walked out of prison.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

Oxford University's Divinity School served as the Hogwarts Hospital Wing, and Duke Humfrey's Library, part of the Bodleian Library, was used as the Hogwarts Library. Christ Church, Oxford was the location for the Hogwarts trophy room.

The Golden Compass

Filming took place in Radcliffe Square, Christ Church and Exeter College.

X-Men: First Class

Principal photography began on August 31, 2010, in Oxford, England, which included St Aldate's street and some of the University of Oxford's buildings, and lasted for two days. An X-men prequel showing Xavier's time at Oxford, Charles drinks at a fake pub by the Bridge of Sighs.

The Riot Club

The film is set among the Riot Club, a fictional all-male, exclusive dining club at the University of Oxford. The shooting of the film began on 30 June 2013 in Oxford, England. The crew were spotted during the filming of scenes at Magpie Lane.

Doctor Strange

In January 2016, filming took place at Exeter College, Oxford.

Transformers: The Last Knight

Filming for Transformers: The Last Knight, which stars Mark Walberg and Anthony Hopkins, took place in Radcliffe Square.

Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again

In 1979, a young Donna graduates from New College, Oxford with Rosie and Tanya, and prepares to travel the world.
